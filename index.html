Getting started
Welcome to nmedea.

nmedea is built differently. Before you dive in, take a moment to understand what makes this platform unlike anything else you have used.

Live viewing on uploaded videos
When you open any video on nmedea — uploaded an hour ago or a day ago — you enter a live session. You can see the exact number of people watching that same video at that very moment. It is not a view count. It is a live headcount, updating in real time.
Live comments — understand this before you type
Every comment on nmedea is live. The moment you send a message, it appears instantly for every person watching that video — no delay, no buffer, no chance to take it back.

How comments work:
Every comment displays the country it was sent from. You can like or dislike any comment and those reactions update live. You can reply directly to other users and those replies are visible in real time. Every 10 comments, the oldest ones automatically disappear — keeping the room in the present moment.

Moderated and regulated:
The comment section is actively regulated. Only one comment can be sent per second per user — flooding is not possible. This keeps conversations meaningful and prevents the section from becoming noise.

Language is not a barrier:
nmedea has a built-in language integration system. Anyone from anywhere in the world can join the same conversation and be understood. A person in South Africa and a person in South Korea can interact in the same comment section, in real time, without confusion. The world is the room.

This is a global public space:
Discrimination of any kind — based on race, nationality, language, religion or gender — is not tolerated. Violations can result in your account being permanently removed without notice. nmedea is built for connection, not division.

What you type is what the world sees — the moment you press send. Be thoughtful. Be respectful.
All posts expire in 24 hours
Content on nmedea does not stay forever. Every post is automatically removed 24 hours after it is uploaded. The platform is designed to live in the present — what you see now will not be here tomorrow. There are no archives. There is only now.
Built on infrastructure unlike any other platform
nmedea runs on a real-time database architecture that processes every single interaction — every view count, comment, like, reply, presence signal and live indicator — simultaneously across the entire platform, for every user, at the same time.

Nothing is cached and served later. Nothing is approximate. Every number you see and every action you take is happening live in the database at that exact moment.

What this costs:
This level of real-time infrastructure is significantly more demanding than conventional social media. Running it is expensive by design — because the experience it creates cannot be replicated any other way. You are not using a standard social platform. The architecture beneath what you see is built for something different.

Guest access and sessions:
You can browse nmedea and watch videos without an account. However, after a period of time the platform will prompt you to sign in or create an account to continue interacting. Commenting, liking, uploading and following all require an account. It is free and takes under a minute.

nmedea is not trying to be like anything else. It is its own thing — and you are seeing it from the very beginning.
You are early — and that matters more than you think
nmedea is new. You may open the app and find that not many videos have been posted yet, or that the comment sections are quiet. That is not a flaw — it is where every great platform begins. Every community that exists today was once exactly this: a small number of people who showed up before everyone else did.

The platform is still being refined. Some things may not work perfectly yet. If something feels off, or you have a thought on what nmedea should become, that feedback is taken seriously and acted on.

The people who join now are the ones who will shape what this becomes. You are one of them.
